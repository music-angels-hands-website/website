## Q1. Could you briefly introduce yourself?
**A.**  
I am a writer who explores the fractures within human existence and the quiet depths of the inner world. I try to give form to emotions that resist language. Writing, for me, is both a way of confronting the world and reconciling with it.

---

## Q2. Why did you choose this profession (writing)?
**A.**  
Since childhood, I have been drawn to what remains unspoken. People speak often, yet in the most crucial moments, they fall silent. I wanted to listen to that silence. Writing became the closest way to approach and hold it.

---

## Q3. What do you consider your greatest strength?
**A.**  
Endurance. I can stay with a feeling or an image for a long time, waiting until it reveals itself fully. Rather than creating something quickly consumed, I am interested in leaving behind a lingering sensation.

---

## Q4. How do you handle difficult situations?
**A.**  
I do not turn away from them. Instead, I move closer. Pain and anxiety grow shapeless when avoided, but when faced directly, they begin to take on language. I endure by searching for that language.

---

## Q5. What is your perspective on teamwork?
**A.**  
Although writing is often solitary, I do not believe it exists in isolation. A text is always in dialogue—with readers, with other works, and with the world itself. In that sense, writing is a quiet but continuous collaboration.

---

## Q6. Where do you see yourself in five years?
**A.**  
I do not measure time in years, but in depth. I hope to be writing with greater clarity and honesty—closer to the core of what it means to be human, even if that core remains elusive.

---

## Q7. Why should we choose you?
**A.**  
Because I am willing to remain with questions that do not have easy answers. I believe meaningful work comes not from certainty, but from the courage to stay with uncertainty—and to shape it into something that can be shared.

---

## Q8. Do you have any final thoughts?
**A.**  
Silence is not empty. It is full of things we have yet to understand. I write to listen more carefully to that silence.
